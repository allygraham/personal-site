News

November 1, 2013

RTI Update

Employers will now be reporting PAYE information to HMRC in real time. You may see this referred to as Real Time Information - or RTI.

This means that employers will have to send details electronically to HMRC every time they pay an employee, at the time they pay them as part of their routine payroll process.

November 3, 2013

Child Benefit Update

You may be liable to this new tax charge if you, or your partner, have an individual income of more than £50,000 and one of you gets Child Benefit or contributions towards the upkeep of a child.

You will need to either keep receiving Child Benefit payments and fill out tax returns or tell the Child Benefit Office that you want to stop receiving Child Benefit payments.

November 5, 2013

Self Assessment Update

Self Assessment involves completing a tax return each year. You show your income and capital gains (profits on the sale of certain assets) and claim tax allowances or reliefs on your tax return.

If you send in a paper tax return, it must reach HMRC by midnight on 31 October. If you miss this deadline you should send your return online.

My name is Linda and I am the owner of Roulston Payroll & Self Assessment Services. I have previously worked for HMRC for 13 years.

A large portion of my time with HMRC was in the Employers Helpline, working with employers advising them on all aspects of being an employer, from registering, to day to day payroll, expenses and benefits, as well as all monthly and yearly returns.

I have great knowledge on Tax and National Insurance, and also the Construction Industry dealing with contractors and subcontractors. I have spent many years on the Self Employment Helpline, guiding individuals through the Self Assessment maze.

I have been asked many times by employers and individuals to work for them, which has influenced my decision to set up Roulston Payroll & Self Assessment Services.

With my extensive knowledge of HMRC and it's processes I provide a first class service, allowing my clients to concentrate on more important issues, like their business, or even time with the family.
